What's Inside

Twelve chapters, no padding, no fluff. Just every important thing there is to know about sardines and why they belong in your regular diet.

Chapter 1

Why Sardines? The Nutrient Density Argument

Omega-3s, vitamin D, B12, calcium, selenium — sardines' nutritional profile compared to every other common food. The data that makes the case.

Chapter 2

The Omega-3 Deep Dive

EPA vs DHA, the Omega-3 Index, what "1.5g per can" actually means for cardiovascular risk. The PREDIMED data, VITAL, REDUCE-IT — what the trials show.

Chapter 3

The Bone Meal Story

Sardines are one of the rare food sources of bioavailable calcium from actual bone. The calcium-vitamin D synergy, bone mineral density data, and why dairy isn't the only answer.

Chapter 4

Vitamin D: The Surprising Source

Two cans of sardines provide roughly 50% of the daily vitamin D most adults need. The latitude problem, the deficiency epidemic, and why sardines are a rare food-based solution.

Chapter 5

Mercury, PCBs, and Contaminants

Why sardines are among the safest fish you can eat. The FDA/EPA tiered guidance explained, sardine contaminant data vs tuna and salmon, and what "low on the food chain" actually means.

Chapter 6

Sustainability: The Full Picture

Sardine fisheries, MSC certifications, the forage fish ecology question, wild vs farmed aquaculture comparison, and how to choose based on where your fish was caught.

Chapter 7

Fresh vs Canned: What the Research Shows

Does canning destroy omega-3s? The processing science, BPA in can linings, oil-packed vs water-packed, and the brands that test best in independent lab work.

Chapter 8

Sardines in the Mediterranean Diet

How sardines fit within the broader dietary pattern with the strongest longevity evidence. Spain, Portugal, Italy, Greece — regional traditions and what they actually eat.

Chapter 9

Cardiovascular Health: Mechanisms and Evidence

Triglyceride reduction, anti-arrhythmic effects, endothelial function — the specific mechanisms by which sardine consumption reduces cardiovascular risk, mechanistically explained.

Chapter 10

Brain Health, Depression, and Inflammation

DHA in grey matter, EPA's role in mood regulation, the omega-3 depression meta-analysis data, and why neurological health is inseparable from dietary fat quality.

Chapter 11

How Much, How Often

Practical dosing guidance based on the Omega-3 Index targets. How many cans per week moves the needle, how to combine with supplementation, and what "therapeutic" looks like in practice.

Chapter 12

30 Ways to Actually Eat Them

If you think sardines only go on crackers, this chapter will change your kitchen. Thirty tested preparations from quick lunches to slow-cooked mains — including several that convert sardine sceptics on first taste.

From Chapter 2: The Omega-3 Deep Dive

"A single 100g can of sardines packed in water delivers approximately 1.5–2g of EPA+DHA combined. To put that in context: the average American's daily EPA+DHA intake from all dietary sources is estimated at around 130mg. One can of sardines delivers 10–15 times the national daily average. In a single meal.

This isn't a marginal nutrition story. It's a population-level deficiency story. And sardines — cheap, shelf-stable, ready to eat — are one of the most practical solutions available to almost anyone."
