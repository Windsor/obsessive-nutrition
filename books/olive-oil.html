The Science of the World's Most Studied Cooking Fat

By Obsessive Nutrition · ~38,000 words · 130+ citations

Extra virgin olive oil sits at the heart of the dietary pattern with the strongest longevity evidence in nutrition science. But up to 80% of what's sold as "extra virgin" fails quality standards. And even genuine EVOO varies wildly in the polyphenol content that drives its health effects.

This book separates fact from marketing: the polyphenol science, the PREDIMED trial data, the fraud problem, how to tell real from fake, what happens when you cook with it, and what daily consumption actually accomplishes.

From Chapter 3: The Oleocanthal Discovery

"In 2005, Gary Beauchamp noticed something at an olive oil tasting in Sicily. The catch in the throat — the pepper-like sting that quality extra virgin olive oil produces — felt familiar. Too familiar. It was the same sensation as swallowing liquid ibuprofen.

Back at the Monell Chemical Senses Center in Philadelphia, his team isolated the compound responsible: oleocanthal. They demonstrated it inhibited the same COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes as ibuprofen. A 50ml serving of a polyphenol-rich EVOO, they calculated, provided approximately 10% of the ibuprofen dose used for adult pain relief.

Ten percent, daily, for a lifetime. That's the kind of chronic low-grade anti-inflammatory effect that doesn't show up in a week-long clinical trial — but might show up in 20-year mortality data."
