# Obsessive Nutrition — Site Plan

## Domain
obsessivenutrition.com (available as of Mar 3, 2026)

## Brand
- **Series name:** The Obsessive's Guide to [Food/Topic]
- **Tagline:** "The science behind what you eat. No hype."
- **Tone:** Rigorous but accessible. Like a brilliant friend who happens to have a PhD in nutrition biochemistry. Data-driven, no fads.
- **Audience:** Health obsessives, biohackers, educated health consumers, people who read PubMed abstracts for fun

## Site Structure

### Homepage
- Hero: Series name + tagline
- Featured books (grid with covers + Amazon links)
- "What makes us different" — depth, science citations, no agenda
- Email capture: "Get a free chapter from The Obsessive's Guide to Sardines"
- Recent blog posts

### Books Page
- One card per book: cover, blurb, Amazon link, key topics covered
- Series roadmap (upcoming titles)

### Blog
- One category per book/topic: Sardines, Olive Oil, Salmon, Turmeric, etc.
- Plus: Gut Health, Liver Health, Skin Health, Brain Health
- Supporting content: "5 things we got wrong about [food]", "The study everyone cites (and why it's misunderstood)", etc.
- SEO goldmine: long-tail health/nutrition keywords

### About
- Who writes this, why, what the methodology is
- Citation policy (all claims linked to studies)

### Free Chapter (lead magnet)
- Email capture → free PDF chapter
- Mailchimp/ConvertKit for list management

## Tech Stack Options
1. **Ghost** (same as PT Brief) — fast to spin up, built-in newsletter, familiar
2. **Webflow** — better design control, good for book-style landing pages
3. **Carrd** (landing page only) + Ghost (blog) — hybrid

**Recommendation:** Ghost. Glenn already knows it, it has built-in email, and the blog+newsletter combo is exactly what's needed. New Ghost instance on r2d2 (port 2372).

## Monetization
1. Amazon KDP affiliate links on all book pages (self-referral on own books = full royalty)
2. Newsletter sponsorships once list hits 500+ (supplement brands, health services)
3. Affiliate: Examine.com, iHerb, Thorne supplements
4. Future: paid "deep research" newsletter tier
