The Book

She restores hidden art for a living.
She's never applied that skill to herself.

Vivienne Cross is an American art restorer working in Lisbon's National Tile Museum. She spends her days peeling back centuries of overpainting to reveal the original beauty beneath. Her personal life is another story — a passionless divorce, a career stalled, and a rich fantasy life she's never once acted on.

Then a colleague mentions a place. A palácio in the Alfama district. A society that's existed since 1793, devoted to one thing: the art of surrender.

Vivienne tells herself she'll only stay for a drink. She stays for Mariana — a Portuguese photographer with warm hands and an unnerving ability to see exactly what Vivienne has spent her whole life concealing.

Floor by floor, touch by touch, Vivienne discovers that the most terrifying thing in the world isn't being seen naked by strangers. It's being seen — truly seen — by someone who wants you exactly as you are.

The Society

Five floors. Each one deeper.

I

O Salão — The Salon

Beautiful, civilized, clothed. The seduction begins with conversation. This is where desire is identified.

II

O Jardim — The Garden

Semi-private rooms with open doorways. You may watch. You may be watched. This is where inhibition is shed.

III

O Labirinto — The Labyrinth

A maze of themed rooms — the library, the bath house, the mirror room. This is where fantasy becomes flesh.

IV

A Câmara — The Chamber

Private suites for negotiated scenes. The most controlled floor, paradoxically the most extreme.

V

O Vazio — The Void

A single vast room. No rules except consent. No structure. No roles. This is where the masks come off. Very few ever reach the Fifth Floor.

"Floor by floor. Touch by touch. Truth by truth."
