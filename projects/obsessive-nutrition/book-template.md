# Obsessive Nutrition — Book Template

Each book follows this structure. Target length: 15,000-25,000 words (KDP sweet spot for non-fiction).

---

## Book Structure

### Front Matter
- Series introduction (1 page — same across all books, updated per edition)
- How to use this book
- A note on evidence quality (explaining RCTs vs observational vs mechanistic)

---

### Chapter 1: Origin Story (~1,500 words)
The cultural and historical context of the food/topic.
- Where did it come from? Who first cultivated/used it?
- Historical medicinal or culinary uses
- How it spread globally
- When/how did science start paying attention?
- Fun/surprising historical facts

*Goal: Hook the reader, establish that this food has depth worth exploring.*

---

### Chapter 2: What It Actually Is (~2,000 words)
The composition and biochemistry.
- Macronutrient profile
- Key micronutrients and their forms (e.g., not just "iron" but heme vs non-heme)
- Bioactive compounds (polyphenols, omega-3s, specific enzymes, etc.)
- Bioavailability — what the body actually absorbs vs what's on the label
- How preparation/cooking affects composition
- Quality variation (wild vs farmed, fresh vs canned, etc.)

*Goal: Give the reader the foundation to understand the research chapters.*

---

### Chapter 3: The Research — What We Know (~4,000 words)
The core science chapter. Organised by health outcome.
- Cardiovascular health
- Metabolic health (blood sugar, insulin, lipids)
- Inflammation and immune function
- Brain health and cognition
- Cancer prevention (with appropriate caveats)
- Gut health and microbiome
- Longevity markers

For each: summarise the best evidence, note study quality, acknowledge conflicting findings.

*Goal: Comprehensive but honest survey of the evidence.*

---

### Chapter 4: The Research — What We Don't Know (~1,500 words)
The limitations chapter — builds trust by being honest.
- Where the research is weak or conflicting
- Common overclaims in the media vs what studies actually show
- Confounding factors in population studies
- What we'd need to know to be more certain
- Ongoing research worth watching

*Goal: Differentiate from hype. Readers appreciate honesty.*

---

### Chapter 5: How Much, How Often, What Form (~2,000 words)
The practical chapter.
- Optimal intake ranges based on available evidence
- Timing considerations (if relevant)
- Best forms/preparations for bioavailability
- Supplements vs whole food (with honest assessment)
- Drug interactions or contraindications to be aware of
- Special populations (pregnancy, elderly, specific conditions)

*Goal: Actionable without being prescriptive.*

---

### Chapter 6: Buying, Storing, Using (~1,500 words)
The practical guide.
- What to look for when buying (quality markers, labelling, certifications)
- How to store properly
- Best culinary uses and preparation methods
- Recipe ideas (3-5 simple, practical ones)
- Cost considerations and where to source

*Goal: Bridge from knowledge to daily life.*

---

### Chapter 7: The Verdict (~500 words)
A clear, honest summary.
- What the evidence actually supports
- Who benefits most
- Red flags or reasons to be cautious
- Our honest assessment (1-10 evidence strength score)

---

### Back Matter
- References (all citations, formatted)
- Further reading (3-5 books/resources)
- About the series
- Other books in the series
- Author note

## Notes for Each Book
- Always include a "Evidence Quality Key" box (RCT = strong, observational = moderate, mechanistic/animal = weak)
- Never make disease treatment claims — "may support", "associated with", "evidence suggests"
- Include a disclaimer: "This book is for informational purposes. Consult a healthcare professional."
- Aim for 15+ references per chapter minimum
