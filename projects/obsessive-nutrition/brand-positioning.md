# Obsessive Nutrition — Brand Positioning

## The Concept
A science-first book series for people who aren't satisfied with "eat more X, it's good for you." Each book is a complete, obsessive deep-dive into a single food or health topic — history, biochemistry, clinical research, practical application, and honest limitations.

## Series Name Options
- **"The Obsessive's Guide to [Food]"** ← recommended — clean, distinctive, scalable
- **"Obsessive Nutrition: [Food]"** ← works as series subtitle/brand
- **"Deep Nutrition Files: [Food]"** ← more clinical, less personality

## Target Reader
- Age 28-55, educated, health-conscious but science-minded
- Frustrated by shallow wellness content and "superfoods" hype
- Reads research papers but wants the synthesis done for them
- Interested in optimisation, longevity, or specific health outcomes
- Likely already takes some supplements, tracks metrics, or follows a protocol
- Comps: readers of Peter Attia, Andrew Huberman, Rhonda Patrick content

## Tagline Options
- *"Go deeper. The science is worth it."*
- *"Every food has a story. We find the science."*
- *"For people who read the footnotes."*
- *"Obsessive about what goes in your body."*

**Recommended:** *"For people who read the footnotes."* — distinctive, signals the depth, has personality.

## Tone Guide
- **Authoritative but accessible** — cite studies, explain mechanisms, but no jargon without definition
- **Honest about limitations** — acknowledge weak or conflicting evidence; this builds trust
- **No hype** — never claim something "cures" or "prevents" without strong evidence
- **Curious, not preachy** — obsessed with the science, not telling people what to do
- **Occasional dry humour** — humans wrote these, not AI wellness robots

## Website
- **Domain:** obsessivenutrition.com (available as of Mar 3, 2026)
- **Platform:** Ghost (consistent with existing infrastructure) or simple static site
- **Content:** Blog with supporting articles per book topic + email list
- **Lead magnet:** Free chapter download or "The Obsessive's Starter Kit" (top 5 evidence-based nutrition principles)

## KDP Strategy
- Price: $4.99-$9.99 ebook, $12.99-$16.99 paperback
- Series page on Amazon linking all books
- Categories: Health/Nutrition, Food Science, Diet & Nutrition
- Keywords: science-based nutrition, evidence-based health, [food] benefits research
- Reviews strategy: ARC copies to relevant subreddits (r/nutrition, r/longevity, r/ScientificNutrition)

## Revenue Streams
1. KDP royalties (primary)
2. obsessivenutrition.com blog → affiliate links (Cronometer, supplement brands, lab testing)
3. Newsletter sponsorships (once 1,000+ subscribers)
4. Potential: paid "deep dive" newsletter tier, or bundled digital packages

## Competitive Advantage
Nobody is doing single-food deep dives at this length and scientific rigour for the general market. Academic texts exist but are inaccessible and expensive. Podcast content (Huberman, etc.) covers similar depth but isn't searchable or scannable. This series fills that gap.
