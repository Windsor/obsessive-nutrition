## Amazon Product Description (Blurb)

**She inherited a farmhouse in Portugal. She didn't inherit the ancient serpent lord living in the basement.**

Maya Chen is done. Done with her cheating ex, her failed research grant, and her career as a marine biologist going nowhere. When she inherits a crumbling quinta in northern Portugal from a grandmother she barely knew, she figures: sell the house, drink some wine, figure out the rest later.

Then she finds the glowing stone in the basement. And the man — the *creature* — guarding it.

**Tristão** has waited 287 years for his fated mate. He's the heir to an ancient race of shapeshifting serpent beings who've lived hidden beneath Portugal's hills since before the Roman conquest. He's powerful, patient, and absolutely certain that the stubborn American scientist who just inherited the house above his lair is *his.*

Maya is absolutely certain he's insane.

She doesn't believe in magic. She doesn't believe in fate. And she doesn't believe a seven-foot snake-man when he tells her his blood has been singing since the moment she walked through the door.

But the Encanto — the mating bond that binds their kind to their destined partners — doesn't care what Maya believes. It only knows what she *wants.*

And what Maya wants, against every rational instinct she has, is to stop running and let the monster in.

## Kindle Unlimited Strategy Notes

- **Strongly recommended for KU.** Monster romance readers are the heaviest KU consumers. Page reads will be the primary income driver.
- **Pricing:** $4.99 ebook, $15.99 paperback (monster romance readers buy physical copies for shelves)
- **Pre-order:** Essential. Set up 3-4 weeks before launch.
- **Rapid release:** The genre rewards speed. Aim for Book 2 within 3-4 months of Book 1.
- **Series page:** Create immediately. Monster romance readers binge — make it easy.
- **Launch strategy:** 
  - Target BookTok monster romance creators for ARC copies
  - Cross-promote in Ruby Dixon, Zoey Draven, C.M. Nascosta reader groups
  - Use "Portuguese mythology" angle as a differentiator — this is a unique hook
- **Cover:** Professional illustration. NOT a photo cover. Monster romance covers tend toward illustrated/artistic. Suggest: serpentine coils around a woman's silhouette, Portuguese tile (azulejo) patterns, dark emerald and gold color scheme. The cover should scream "monster romance" at thumbnail size.
- **Special editions:** Monster romance readers are collectors. Plan for: signed paperbacks, sprayed edges, hardcover with dust jacket for later.
