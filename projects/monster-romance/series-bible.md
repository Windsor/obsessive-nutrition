# The Enchanted Ones — Series Bible

## Series: A Moura's Claim

*Monster romance rooted in Portuguese mythology. American heroines. Ancient monsters. A hidden world beneath the hills of Portugal.*

---

## Series Concept

Beneath the rolling hills of northern Portugal, an ancient race survives — the **Mouros Encantados**, shapeshifting beings from pre-Roman Iberia who were cursed underground when the Christian conquest swept the peninsula. Part serpent, part human, bound to the land by old magic, they've lived in hidden kingdoms beneath dolmens, castles, and river caves for a thousand years.

Now the old wards are failing. The boundary between worlds is thinning. And certain humans — those with **sangue antigo** (old blood, distant Mouro ancestry) — are being drawn to Portugal by a pull they can't explain.

Each book follows an American woman who discovers she carries old blood, is pulled into the hidden world, and is claimed by a Mouro lord through the **Encanto** — a magical resonance that binds fated mates. Part mating bond, part curse, part gift. When it strikes, both feel it: a warmth in the blood, a hum in the bones, an irresistible pull toward the other.

The Mouros can shift between human-passing form and their true form — taller, scaled along the spine and forearms, slit-pupiled golden or amber eyes, elongated canines, and in their most ancient bloodlines, partial serpentine features (tails, coils, forked tongues). They're beautiful and terrifying. They smell like stone and wildflowers. Their blood runs warm as heated wine.

## The World

### The Hidden Kingdom: **Sob a Pedra** ("Beneath the Stone")

A vast underground realm stretching beneath the Minho and Trás-os-Montes regions of northern Portugal. Accessed through:
- **Dolmens** (ancient stone tombs) — the original gateways
- **Castle ruins** — built over Mouro access points
- **River caves** — especially along the Cávado, Lima, and Douro rivers
- **The roots of ancient oak trees** — living gateways that shift with the seasons

The kingdom is beautiful — bioluminescent caverns, underground rivers that glow silver, vast halls carved from granite, gardens lit by captured starlight. Not a dungeon. A palace beneath the earth.

### Mouro Society

- **Ruled by the Rei das Pedras** (King of Stones) — currently aging, no heir, succession crisis
- **Seven noble houses**, each tied to a region and an element:
  - **Casa da Serpente** (House of the Serpent) — the royal house, most powerful, most ancient
  - **Casa do Rio** (House of the River) — water-aligned, diplomats and traders
  - **Casa da Serra** (House of the Mountain) — warriors, guardians of the borders
  - **Casa do Carvalho** (House of the Oak) — healers, keepers of the living gateways
  - **Casa da Névoa** (House of the Mist) — spies, seers, manipulators
  - **Casa do Fogo** (House of Fire) — smiths, craftspeople, volatile temperaments
  - **Casa da Lua** (House of the Moon) — scholars, historians, keepers of the old language
- **The Encanto** — the mating bond. When a Mouro encounters their fated mate, their blood sings. Literally — the old magic resonates, audible as a low hum that builds to a roar. Cannot be faked or forced. Extremely rare. A Mouro who finds their Encanto partner will do ANYTHING to keep them.
- **Shifting** — all Mouros can shift between a human-passing form and their true form. The shift reveals: scaled patches along spine/forearms/ribs, slit pupils, elongated canines, clawed fingertips. The most powerful bloodlines have additional features — partial coils (serpentine lower body), forked tongues, the ability to grow to enormous size.
- **Lifespan** — centuries. A Mouro in their prime might be 300 years old. They age slowly after maturity.
- **Weakness** — iron (burns them), church bells (disorienting), and prolonged sunlight weakens their magic (they can endure it, but prefer twilight, night, and underground).

### The Surface World

Modern-day Portugal. The Mouros have agents on the surface — human families who've served as intermediaries for generations. Some know the truth; most think they're preserving "folklore." The tourist industry around castles and dolmens unknowingly protects Mouro access points.

The Mouros occasionally visit the surface in human-passing form. They find modern humans... entertaining. And baffling. Smartphones are regarded with deep suspicion. Coffee, however, is universally beloved (they discovered it before the humans did, they claim).

### The Threat

The **Adamastor** — the ancient giant from Portuguese legend — is real. Imprisoned beneath the Atlantic by the Mouros millennia ago. The failing wards that separate the hidden kingdom from the surface world are ALSO weakening Adamastor's prison. If he breaks free, both worlds burn.

This is the series arc: each book's couple must secure one of the seven ward-stones that keep Adamastor bound, while falling in love despite impossible odds.

## The Encanto (Mating Bond) — Details

### How It Works
- **Trigger:** Physical proximity between a Mouro and their fated mate. Can happen at first sight or first touch.
- **Mouro experience:** A roaring heat in the blood. Their scales flush gold. Their eyes burn. Every instinct screams MINE. They can feel their mate's heartbeat, emotional state, and location.
- **Human experience:** A warmth that spreads from the chest. A hum they can't explain — like standing near a power line. Vivid dreams of the Mouro in their true form. Increasing sensitivity to touch. An inexplicable pull toward a place (Portugal) or a person.
- **Completion:** The bond is sealed through mutual claiming — sex with intent, spoken vows in the old language, and an exchange of blood (a bite, traditionally on the throat). Once sealed, both gain: extended lifespan, shared emotional awareness, the ability to communicate without words in close proximity.
- **Rejection:** Theoretically possible. A Mouro who is rejected by their Encanto mate will survive but never bond again. They become "Sem Luz" (Without Light) — diminished, cold, slowly fading. It's considered the worst fate.

### During Sex
- The Mouro's scales emerge involuntarily during arousal — they can't hide their true form when the Encanto is active
- The bond amplifies sensation — both partners feel echoes of the other's pleasure
- The "hum" becomes audible — a low vibration that builds with arousal
- At climax, the Mouro's eyes glow gold and the nearest stone/earth trembles
- Temperature rises — Mouro blood runs hot; the human partner feels feverish

## Book 1: ENCANTADA

### Premise

**Maya Chen**, 28, a burned-out marine biologist from Boston, inherits a crumbling quinta (farmhouse) in the Minho region from a grandmother she barely knew. She comes to Portugal planning to sell the property, drink some wine, and figure out her life after a messy breakup and a failed research grant.

Instead, she discovers the quinta sits directly above one of the seven ward-stones — and the Mouro lord tasked with guarding it has been waiting for her grandmother's bloodline to return for sixty years.

**Tristão** is the heir to Casa da Serpente — the royal house, the most powerful Mouro bloodline. He's 287 years old, lethal, coldly beautiful in his human form, and absolutely terrifying in his true form (eight feet tall, scaled from the waist down into a massive serpentine coil, golden slit-pupiled eyes, fangs). He's also exhausted. He's been guarding the ward-stone beneath Maya's quinta alone for decades because the Encanto bond his bloodline needs to unlock the stone has never triggered — until Maya walks through the door of her grandmother's house and his blood ignites.

The problem: Maya is the most stubbornly rational person he's ever met. She doesn't believe in magic. She doesn't believe in fate. And she definitely doesn't believe the seven-foot snake-man in her basement when he tells her they're destined mates.

### Tone

Humor + heat + heart. Maya's wry, skeptical narration against Tristão's ancient, intense POV. Fish-out-of-water (American in rural Portugal) meets monster-out-of-water (ancient being dealing with a woman who argues with destiny). Think Ruby Dixon's humor + Zoey Draven's worldbuilding + Grace Draven's prose quality.

### Key Tropes
- Fated mates / Encanto bond
- Size difference (he's massive in true form)
- Grumpy/sunshine (she's the sunshine, he's the grumpy ancient serpent)
- Fish out of water (double — her in Portugal, him dealing with a modern human)
- Forced proximity (she literally lives above his lair)
- "You're not a monster" moment
- Inheritance/old house mystery
- Touch starved (he's been alone for decades)
- He falls first (hard, immediately, the Encanto wrecks him)

### POV & Tense

First person, present tense, dual POV (Maya / Tristão), alternating chapters.

### Chapter Outline — 30 Chapters + Epilogue

**Act 1: Arrival (Ch 1-8)**

1. **MAYA** — Arrives in Portugal. Rental car on narrow roads. The quinta is beautiful but decrepit. Finds grandmother's journal with strange drawings of serpents. First night alone, hears humming from the basement. Dismisses it.
2. **TRISTÃO** — Has been guarding the ward-stone for 63 years. Feels the old blood arrive. The Encanto hits him like a lightning strike. He shifts involuntarily, scales flushing gold. After decades of silence, his blood is ROARING. He needs to see her.
3. **MAYA** — Explores the quinta. Meets the nearest neighbor, **Dona Celeste** (80s, hilarious, knows about the Mouros but plays dumb). Discovers the basement has a locked stone door she can't open. That night, dreams of golden eyes and warm stone.
4. **TRISTÃO** — Watches her from the shadows (creepy, but he can't help it). She's nothing like he expected — argues with furniture, talks to herself, eats cereal for dinner. She's beautiful and infuriating and his blood won't stop singing. He decides to approach in human form.
5. **MAYA** — A man appears at her door. Tall, dark-haired, amber eyes, absurdly beautiful. Says he's a neighbor, offers to help with the property. His name is Tristão. She's suspicious (nobody is this helpful) but lonely. He flinches when she accidentally touches his hand — and she feels a jolt of heat. Weird.
6. **TRISTÃO** — Maintaining human form around her is agony. Every instinct screams to reveal himself, to claim, to bite, to coil around her and never let go. Instead he's... helping her fix a leaking roof. This is humiliating. He's the heir to Casa da Serpente and he's grouting tiles.
7. **MAYA** — Finds more of grandmother's journal. Drawings of the hidden kingdom. References to "o encantado" and "sangue antigo." Thinks it's folklore. Meanwhile, Tristão keeps showing up. She's attracted to him but wary. She notices he avoids sunlight and the church bell in the village makes him wince.
8. **TRISTÃO** — The ward-stone is weakening faster. He can feel tremors deep in the earth — Adamastor stirring. He needs Maya's old blood to help strengthen the ward, but she doesn't know what she is. He's running out of time to be gentle about this.

**Act 2: Revelation (Ch 9-16)**

9. **MAYA** — Earthquake. Small, but the basement door cracks open. She goes in with a flashlight and finds a vast cavern beneath the quinta. In the center, a glowing stone covered in ancient symbols. She reaches out to touch it — and the stone RESPONDS. Light floods through her. She passes out.
10. **TRISTÃO** — He catches her before she hits the ground. She saw the ward-stone. She touched it. She's alive, which means the old blood is strong. He carries her upstairs and agonizes — tell her the truth or protect her from it? The tremor decides for him. Something in the deep shifts. He can't keep lying.
11. **MAYA** — Wakes up in bed. Tristão is sitting by the window, and in the moonlight, his eyes are... wrong. Gold and slit-pupiled. She demands the truth. He gives it. Shifts into his true form right in her bedroom. Eight feet of scaled serpentine power. She screams. (Fair.)
12. **TRISTÃO** — She threw a lamp at him. He deserved it. Now she's locked herself in the bathroom and is calling him "a fucking hallucination." He sits outside the door and explains everything — the Mouros, the hidden kingdom, her grandmother, the old blood, the Encanto. He leaves out the mating bond part. One crisis at a time.
13. **MAYA** — She doesn't believe him. Then she does, because science brain kicks in — the stone responded to her touch, his transformation is physically impossible but she SAW it, and grandmother's journal suddenly makes sense. She opens the bathroom door. He's back in human form, looking wrecked. "Show me again," she says. He does. She studies him like a specimen. He finds this both insulting and arousing.
14. **TRISTÃO** — She wants to take SAMPLES. Of his SCALES. He is the heir to Casa da Serpente, and this woman wants to put him under a microscope. He refuses. She argues. He's furious. He's enchanted. She's everything he never expected and nothing he can control.
15. **MAYA** — He takes her underground. The hidden kingdom. She sees the bioluminescent caverns, the underground rivers, the ancient halls. It's the most beautiful thing she's ever seen. She meets other Mouros — warriors from Casa da Serra, a healer from Casa do Carvalho. They stare at her like she's a miracle. She realizes she's the first human down here in decades.
16. **TRISTÃO** — The Rei das Pedras (king) is failing. The court demands to know why Tristão brought a human below. He tells them: the ward-stone responded to her. She carries old blood. The court is split — some see hope, others see a threat. Tristão swears on his bloodline to protect her. The old vow binds him. He doesn't tell them about the Encanto. That's his.

**Act 3: Ignition (Ch 17-22)**

17. **MAYA** — Something is changing in her body. Her skin feels electric. She's hyperaware of Tristão — can feel when he's near, like a magnet in her chest. Dreams of golden scales and warm coils around her body. She tells herself it's stress. Dona Celeste, the neighbor, stops by and casually mentions: "Your grandmother felt it too. The pull. She fought it for years." Maya is NOT reassured.
18. **TRISTÃO** — He can't sleep. The Encanto is a constant roar. He can feel her heartbeat from three rooms away. When she brushes past him in the hallway, his scales ripple beneath his skin. He's losing control. He tells himself he'll explain the bond properly. Tomorrow. Always tomorrow.
19. **MAYA** — They're in the cavern together, working on the ward-stone (her scientific approach + his ancient knowledge). Their hands touch over the stone. The hum becomes a ROAR. Light floods from the stone. She looks at him and sees his eyes blazing gold, scales cascading down his neck, and she feels it — the Encanto, like a second heartbeat slamming into her chest. "What the fuck is that?" she whispers.
20. **TRISTÃO** — He tells her. Everything. The Encanto. Fated mates. What it means. She's furious — at him for hiding it, at fate for deciding for her, at herself for wanting it anyway. She storms out. He lets her go. He can feel her anger through the bond like broken glass in his bloodstream.
21. **MAYA** — She sits on the quinta's terrace, overlooking the Minho valley at sunset, and calls her best friend **Jade** back in Boston. Tries to explain. Can't. Hangs up. Dona Celeste appears with wine and pastéis de nata. The old woman tells her: "Your grandmother loved him too. She just couldn't stay." Maya: "Why not?" Celeste: "Because she was afraid. Not of him. Of wanting him more than the life she'd built." Maya realizes she doesn't have a life she'd be losing. Not anymore.
22. **TRISTÃO** — She comes to him. Not to the quinta — she descends to HIS space, the cavern below. She finds him in true form, coiled and miserable. "I'm not my grandmother," she says. "I don't run." She touches his face. His scales. He breaks. **First sex scene.** He shifts between forms — starts human, loses control, scales emerge, the coil wraps around her. She doesn't flinch. The Encanto roars. The stone glows. The earth hums.

**Act 4: Complication (Ch 23-26)**

23. **MAYA** — The morning after. She wakes wrapped in his coils, warm and safe. She should be freaking out. She's not. The Encanto is a steady pulse between them now — she can feel his contentment, his awe, his terror of losing her. She has a serpent boyfriend. Cool. Cool cool cool. She also realizes the ward-stone is STRONGER — their coupling near it actually reinforced the magic. Science brain goes wild.
24. **TRISTÃO** — The court finds out about the Encanto. **Iago**, lord of Casa da Névoa, challenges Tristão's claim — argues a human mate weakens the bloodline. Iago wants the throne and sees Maya as a vulnerability. Political danger. Meanwhile, the tremors are increasing. Adamastor is waking.
25. **MAYA** — Iago's people try to scare her off — illusions, threats, a shadow creature sent to her bedroom. Tristão goes nuclear. She sees the killer in him for the first time. He destroys the shadow creature with terrifying ease. She realizes he's been holding back around her — what she's seen is a fraction of his power.
26. **TRISTÃO** — He goes to Iago. The confrontation is cold, political, and deadly. Tristão makes clear: touch his mate, and Casa da Névoa ceases to exist. Iago backs down — for now. But the damage is done. Maya saw what Tristão is capable of. He waits for her to recoil.

**Act 5: Claiming (Ch 27-30 + Epilogue)**

27. **MAYA** — She doesn't recoil. She's a marine biologist — she's studied apex predators her entire career. She knows what danger looks like. She also knows Tristão would never turn that on her. She tells him this, plainly, rationally, the way she does everything. He stares at her like she hung the stars. **Second sex scene** — slower, more tender, she initiates. She explores his true form — every scale, every coil. He's shaking. Nobody has ever touched him like this.
28. **TRISTÃO** — The final tremor. Adamastor's prison cracks. The ward-stone begins to fail. They need to seal it NOW — both of them, together. He tells Maya the sealing requires the full Encanto bond: spoken vows in the old language, an exchange of blood, a claiming that cannot be undone. She'll be bound to him. Centuries of life. She'll never fully be human again.
29. **MAYA** — The choice. Not between Tristão and her old life — she already chose him. Between being Maya Chen, human, and becoming something new. Something ancient and modern at once. She thinks of her grandmother, who ran. She thinks of the life she left in Boston — empty apartment, failed research, no one waiting. She thinks of golden eyes and warm stone and a heartbeat that matches hers. "Teach me the words."
30. **TRISTÃO** — The sealing. Ward-stone cavern. Ancient words. His bite on her throat (she bites back, because of course she does). The ward-stone BLAZES. Adamastor's assault is repelled — for now. One stone of seven, secured. The kingdom celebrates. Tristão holds his mate and feels, for the first time in 287 years, that the future might be worth seeing. **Third sex scene** — celebratory, intense, fully bonded (she feels HIS pleasure now too), the earth literally trembles.

**EPILOGUE — MAYA** — Three months later. She's set up a marine biology research station at a coastal cave that connects to the hidden kingdom. She's studying the bioluminescent organisms in the underground rivers (they're not from this world). Tristão brings her coffee — he's learned to use an espresso machine, which he considers his greatest achievement. Dona Celeste visits and brings pastéis de nata. Jade is coming to visit from Boston — Maya has NO idea how to explain any of this. A letter arrives from Casa do Rio, the second noble house: their lord has felt an Encanto. With an American woman who just booked a walking tour of the Camino de Santiago. The next ward-stone is in his territory. **Book 2 hook.**

Healer lord. Ward-stone in an ancient oak forest. She's terminally ill; the Encanto offers a cure — but at what cost?

Final book. Scholar lord. The last ward-stone. Adamastor's final assault. All couples return for the climax. Epic finale.

## Character Details — Book 1

### Maya Chen, 28
- **Background:** Chinese-American, raised in Boston by immigrant parents. Marine biologist (PhD from MIT/WHOI joint program). Research focused on bioluminescent deep-sea organisms.
- **Why she's in Portugal:** Inherited the quinta from her maternal grandmother, Avó Lúcia, who she barely knew. Grandmother left Boston decades ago and never came back. Maya's mother refused to talk about it.
- **Personality:** Wry, skeptical, stubborn, curious above all else. Science is her religion. She processes emotions through analysis. Not cold — warm underneath, but armored. The breakup and career failure have her questioning everything.
- **Voice:** Quick, funny, self-deprecating. Pop culture references. Swears freely. Undercuts tension with humor.
- **Arc:** From "rational woman who controls everything" to "woman who chooses the uncontrollable — love, magic, a new life."

### Tristão, 287 (looks ~32)
- **Background:** Heir to Casa da Serpente, the royal house. Son of the ailing Rei das Pedras. Has spent 63 years guarding the ward-stone alone — a self-imposed exile after his father's health failed and the court descended into political infighting.
- **Human form:** Tall (6'4"), lean, angular features, warm brown skin, dark hair, amber eyes that catch the light wrong. Moves like something predatory pretending to be casual. Speaks Portuguese, English, French, Latin, and the old Mouro tongue.
- **True form:** Eight feet tall. Scaled from the waist down into a powerful serpentine coil (12+ feet when extended). Scales are deep emerald and gold. Upper body remains humanoid but larger, with scales along the spine, forearms, and ribs. Eyes blaze gold. Fangs. Clawed fingertips. Forked tongue (which he's embarrassed about around Maya until she finds it... interesting).
- **Personality:** Controlled, dry-humored, deeply lonely. Formal speech patterns that crack when emotional. Possessive once the Encanto hits, but fights it because he wants her to CHOOSE him, not be compelled. Proud but vulnerable underneath.
- **Voice:** More formal, archaic phrasing that modernizes as he spends time with Maya. Sardonic. Occasionally devastating with a single quiet sentence.
- **Arc:** From "isolated guardian who's forgotten how to want things" to "man (monster) who discovers wanting is the point."

### Supporting Cast
- **Dona Celeste** — 80s, Maya's neighbor, knows everything, says nothing useful until it's dramatic. Brings food constantly. The Portuguese grandmother everyone deserves.
- **Jade Thompson** — Maya's best friend in Boston. Black, outspoken, a lawyer. Will be Book 2's heroine. Video-calls Maya frequently and provides comic relief.
- **Iago** — Lord of Casa da Névoa. The antagonist. Silver-tongued, politically dangerous, genuinely believes humans weaken Mouro bloodlines. Gets his own redemption in Book 5.
- **The Rei das Pedras** — Tristão's father. Dying. Wise but fading. His final wish is to see his son bonded before he passes.

## Portuguese Cultural Elements to Include

- **Food:** Pastéis de nata, caldo verde, francesinha, bacalhau, vinho verde. Mouros have their own cuisine (underground mushrooms, river fish, honey wine).
- **Landscape:** Minho's green terraced vineyards, granite villages, Atlantic mist, dolmens, castle ruins.
- **Language:** Sprinkle Portuguese throughout — terms of endearment (minha luz, querida), Mouro-specific vocabulary (Encanto, sangue antigo, Sob a Pedra), place names.
- **Music:** Fado as a human echo of Mouro mourning songs. The Mouros have their own music — deep, resonant, felt in the bones.
- **Saudade:** The untranslatable longing. Central to Tristão's character — 287 years of saudade for a mate he'd never met.
- **Festivals:** Incorporate São João (Porto's festival) or local romarias.
