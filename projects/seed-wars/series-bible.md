# SEED WARS — Series Bible

## Series Concept
An unknown galactic organization (The Wardens) sends biological "seeds" to primitive planets targeted by a locust-like alien invasion (The Swarm). These seeds bond with select humans, granting superhuman abilities — a last-ditch inoculation strategy to give doomed worlds a fighting chance. The Wardens have done this to thousands of worlds. Some survived. Most didn't.

## Book 1: "THE GERMINATION"
**Timeline:** One year before the invasion. Seeds land → powers manifest → chaos → invasion begins (cliffhanger ending).
**Tone:** Adult, gritty, action-packed. Think The Boys meets Independence Day meets X-Men.
**POV:** Third-person limited, rotating between 4-5 main characters.
**Target:** ~40,000 words, 30 chapters.

## The Seeds
- Arrive as a meteor shower, thousands of small bioluminescent pods
- Most burn up in atmosphere; ~200 reach the surface worldwide
- Seeds seek compatible hosts within a 1km radius — burrow into skin on contact
- Bonding is painful, takes 24-72 hours of fever/hallucinations
- Each seed grants unique abilities based on the host's psychology, physiology, and deepest desires/fears
- Powers escalate over weeks/months — starts small, grows dangerous
- Some hosts reject the seed — they die horribly
- Seeds leave a faint bioluminescent mark on the host's skin (a spiral pattern)

## The Swarm (revealed only at the very end of Book 1)
- Locust-like alien species that strips planets of all biological resources
- Hive-mind, billions strong, travel in massive organic ships
- They've consumed thousands of worlds
- First sign: a shadow crossing the moon in the final chapter

## Main Characters

### 1. MARCUS COLE (34) — Former Army Ranger, now construction worker in Detroit
- **Power:** Kinetic absorption and redirection. Can absorb any physical force and release it.
- **Arc:** Reluctant hero. Divorced, estranged daughter (Lily, 8). Drinks too much. The seed forces him sober — alcohol disrupts his power. Becomes the closest thing to a leader among the "Seeded."
- **Personality:** Quiet, competent, haunted by what he did overseas. Dry humor.

### 2. ELENA VASQUEZ (28) — ER nurse in Miami
- **Power:** Biokinesis — can manipulate biological matter. Healing at first, then realizes she can also destroy: give tumors, stop hearts, reshape flesh.
- **Arc:** Starts as a healer, beloved by her community. The temptation of her destructive potential grows. A cartel kidnaps her family to force her to work for them. She does terrible things to get them back.
- **Personality:** Warm, fierce, Catholic guilt about her power.

### 3. TREVOR HAINES (22) — College dropout, incel-adjacent loner in Portland
- **Power:** Telepathy and psychic domination. Can read minds and, eventually, control them.
- **Arc:** THE VILLAIN (or one of them). Starts sympathetic — bullied, lonely, invisible. Power corrupts absolutely. Builds a cult. Does horrifying things. Represents the worst-case scenario of seeds landing on the wrong person.
- **Personality:** Intelligent, resentful, increasingly narcissistic and cruel.

### 4. AMARA OSEI (40) — Ghanaian-British MI6 analyst stationed in London
- **Power:** Technopathy — can interface with and control any electronic system with her mind.
- **Arc:** The one who figures out the bigger picture. Tracks the meteor shower, notices the pattern of powered individuals, starts connecting dots. Government wants to weaponize the Seeded. She has to choose between duty and doing right.
- **Personality:** Brilliant, controlled, dry wit, chess player. Divorced, wife got custody.

### 5. JAKE MERCER (45) — US Senator from Texas
- **Power:** Superhuman charisma/pheromone manipulation — can make anyone trust/obey him in person.
- **Arc:** Already powerful, now unstoppable. Uses his power to fast-track himself toward the presidency. Publicly champions "Seeded rights" while privately building a power base to control them. The political villain.
- **Personality:** Charming, calculated, genuinely believes he's the right person to lead humanity.

## Supporting Characters
- **Dr. Yuki Tanaka** — Japanese geneticist, studies the seeds, discovers they're engineered
- **Lily Cole** — Marcus's daughter, becomes his motivation
- **Father Ramón** — Elena's priest, helps her grapple with morality
- **"The Collective"** — Trevor's growing cult of mind-controlled followers
- **General Patricia Stone** — Amara's superior, hawkish, wants Seeded as weapons
- **Sarah Chen** — Journalist documenting the Seeded phenomenon

## Timeline (Book 1)

### Act 1: Germination (Chapters 1-10)
- The meteor shower (spectacular global event)
- Seeds find hosts over the following days
- First manifestations — confusion, fear, wonder
- World reacts: scientists baffled, religious groups call it divine/demonic
- Government scrambles
- Characters discover their powers independently
- First Seeded death (rejection) makes the news

### Act 2: Growth (Chapters 11-20)
- Powers escalate — some go public, some hide
- Marcus accidentally kills someone with his power, goes on the run
- Elena becomes an underground healer, draws cartel attention
- Trevor starts small (making bullies humiliate themselves), escalates to cult leader
- Amara discovers the seeds are engineered — not natural
- Senator Mercer rises, pushes "Seeded Registration Act"
- First major Seeded-vs-Seeded conflict
- Government black sites for captured Seeded
- Underground railroad for Seeded who don't want to register

### Act 3: Bloom (Chapters 21-30)
- Trevor's cult commits a massacre — public turns against all Seeded
- Elena's family kidnapped, she goes full dark to save them
- Marcus pulled into conflict with Trevor — epic showdown
- Amara uncovers that the meteor shower's trajectory was GUIDED — someone sent the seeds deliberately
- Mercer consolidates power, wins emergency presidential authority
- Marcus and Amara connect, share intel
- Elena emerges from her darkness, scarred but alive
- Trevor defeated but not killed — escapes
- Mercer's true nature exposed to our heroes but not the public
- FINAL CHAPTER: Characters in different locations all look up at the sky. Something massive is blocking out the stars. The invasion has arrived. End.

## Themes
- Power corrupts — but it also reveals who you already were
- The best intentions of unknown benefactors don't account for human nature
- Unity vs. tribalism when facing extinction
- The surveillance state's response to superhumans
- What you do when no one can stop you defines you

## World Rules
- ~200 Seeded worldwide (not all survive bonding)
- Powers are unique but can be categorized (physical, mental, energy, biological)
- No one knows where the seeds came from (Book 1)
- The Wardens are never named or seen in Book 1 — only hinted at through seed engineering
- The Swarm appears only in the final pages
